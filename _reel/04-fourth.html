Examples from the world

The Berlin Wall is the most famous example, the wall was built in 1961 to separate East and West Berlin. After the fall, in 1990 artists from all over the world began to paint the wall, 160 paintings, 118 artists from 21 countries of the world. This is the largest exhibition dedicated to freedom and peace.





Lake Street tunnel, is a road tunnel in Lambert, central London. Its history began before 1920, this place has always enjoyed a not very good reputation, from early morning the bars next to this tunnel were filled, different variations of cheap alcohol attracted people’s attention, the gloomy and chaotically painted place had a reputation for insanity. This was the case until 2008, when a well-known street artist, Benxi, carried out a renovation, he used the history and disadvantages of this place, changed them into pluses and opened a new space for self-expression. Now it is a 300-meter canvas for street art artists. More than 1 million tourists visit this place every year, as well as locals and tourists often like to arrange performances and photo shoots there.




Prague also does not lag behind the world capitals, street art is very common here. The most famous is the John Lenon Wall located in the heart of the Old Town, a few steps from the Charles Bridge. In 1988, Czech youth began to use this revenge to express their thoughts about the harsh communist realities. Today, Lennon’s wall is filled with messages of love and peace, and the portrait of the famous musician has long been covered with several layers of original drawings.


Photo wallpaper in memory of Bogumil Graban, Bogumil Grabal is one of the most important figures in Czech and world literature. His books have been translated into 25 languages, several of them have been filmed. Located right at the exit of the Palmovka metro station.


The Zizkov district and the Florentz station area are overflowing with works of street art.
